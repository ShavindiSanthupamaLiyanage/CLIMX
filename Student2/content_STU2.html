The Link Between Climate Change & Public Health

Climate change is not just an environmental issue, it is a serious public health crisis. Rising global temperatures, extreme weather events, air pollution, and shifting disease patterns are directly affecting human health worldwide.

The 2023 heatwaves in Europe led to over 61,000 deaths due to extreme heat exposure.

Air pollution from wildfires in Canada spread toxic smoke across the USA, worsening respiratory illnesses like asthma.

Major Health Impacts of Climate Change

Extreme Heat & Heat-Related Illnesses

Rising temperatures lead to heat exhaustion, dehydration, and heat strokes.

Vulnerable populations, such as the elderly and outdoor workers, are at higher risk.

In 2023, Europe recorded its hottest summer, causing 61,000 heat-related deaths.

Air Pollution & Respiratory Diseases

Burning fossil fuels releases harmful gases, leading to air pollution-related diseases.

Asthma, lung cancer, and cardiovascular diseases are rising due to polluted air.

Studies show that air pollution causes 7 million deaths annually, according to the WHO.

Wildfire smoke in Canada (2023) caused a major air quality crisis in the US, increasing asthma-related hospital visits by 30%.

Transition to renewable energy sources like wind and solar power to reduce air pollution.

Enforce stricter air pollution regulations.

Vector-Borne Diseases (Malaria, Dengue, Zika)

Rising temperatures and humidity create the perfect conditions for mosquitoes to thrive.

Malaria and dengue cases are increasing in regions where they were previously uncommon.

Severe droughts in Africa have put 20 million people at risk of starvation.

Invest in mosquito control programs.

Improve disease surveillance systems.

Water and Food Insecurity

Climate change disrupts agriculture and drinking water sources, leading to malnutrition and foodborne illnesses.

Severe droughts in Africa have put 20 million people at risk of starvation.

Promote climate-smart farming.

Increase water conservation efforts.

Vulnerable Populations at Higher Risk

While climate change affects everyone, certain vulnerable populations are disproportionately impacted due to age, health conditions, socio-economic status, and geographic location. These groups face higher exposure to environmental risks and have fewer resources to cope with climate-related disasters.

Hurricane Maria (2017) in Puerto Rico led to over 3,000 deaths, mainly among the elderly and people in poverty.

Children are especially vulnerable to climate change due to their developing immune systems and higher exposure to pollutants. They breathe more air relative to their body size, making them more susceptible to air pollution-related diseases like asthma. In heavily polluted areas like Delhi, India, asthma rates among children have increased by 50% over the past two decades due to vehicle emissions and industrial pollution. Additionally, climate-induced droughts in Africa and South Asia have led to food shortages, increasing child malnutrition and causing long-term developmental issues. To protect children, governments need to implement stricter air pollution control policies and improve nutrition programs in high-risk areas.
